Introduction

Recent advancements in Multi-Modal Large Language Models (MLLMs) have driven the development of complex reasoning tasks integrating text, images, and structured data, spanning Logical Reasoning, Mathematical Reasoning, Spatio- Temporal Understanding, Coding, Map Navigation, and Scientific Reasoning. However, the reasoning capabilities of MLLMs, particularly those with Think- ing (MLLMs-T), remain underexplored, lacking a standardized evaluation frame- work. We introduce MMMR, a novel benchmarking framework designed to evaluate MLLMs-T and MLLMs through a three-stage pipeline: 1) Reasoning Dataset Con- struction, creating the MMMR with diverse multi-modal tasks; 2) Thinking Quality Assessment, using metrics like RTQ, RTA, RSC, Acc, TLen, and OA to assess reasoning coherence and alignment; and 3) Insight Synthesis, deriving actionable strategies for improvement. Our evaluation on the MMMR reveals: 1) MLLMs- T outperform MLLMs in multi-modal reasoning, especially in tasks requiring modality integration; 2) Thinking quality assessment identifies prevalent errors like repetitive thinking and overthinking, guiding targeted enhancements; and 3) synthesized insights suggest improvements in reasoning coherence, multi-modal in- tegration, and efficiency, addressing overthinking via optimized Thinking strategies. These findings provide a foundation for advancing MLLMs-T, and we advocate for further research to enhance scalability, robustness, and real-world applica- bility of multi-modal reasoning systems.

MMMR Dataset

MMMR establishes a comprehensive reasoning dataset MMMR to evaluate the multi-modal reasoning capabilities of MLLMs-T. It encompasses six task types‚ÄîLogical Reasoning, Mathematical Reasoning, Spatio-Temporal Understanding, Coding, Map Navigation, and Scientific Reasoning‚Äîincorporating subcategories such as deductive reasoning, algebraic problem-solving, temporal analysis, code generation, path planning, and scientific hypothesis testing. MMMR integrates diverse modalities, including textual descriptions, images, and structured data (e.g., tables, graphs), with a balanced distribution across difficulty levels. MMMR is partitioned into validation and test sets to ensure a thorough and reproducible assessment of model performance.
